The Problem: A Market Tilted Against You

1. Vulnerability to Sophisticated Scams

Retail investors are the primary targets of coordinated "pump and dump" schemes, where manipulators create artificial hype to inflate a stock's price before crashing it by selling their shares, leading to huge losses for others.

What is a Pump & Dump Scheme?

The Pump: Manipulators buy a cheap stock and aggressively promote it with false promises online, tricking others into buying and driving the price up.

The Dump: The manipulators sell all their shares at the inflated price, causing the stock to crash and leaving new investors with massive losses.

2. Information Overload & Noise

It is impossible for an individual to manually sift through thousands of social media posts to distinguish genuine discussion from fraudulent promotion. The sheer volume of data is overwhelming.

3. Lack of Accessible Verification Tools

An investor might see a "hot stock tip" online but has no quick or reliable way to verify its credibility or check if a sudden surge in positive sentiment is organic or artificially manufactured.

4. Prohibitive Cost of Pro-Grade Tools

The powerful surveillance and analysis platforms used by regulators and hedge funds are completely out of reach for the average person, costing lakhs of rupees annually.
